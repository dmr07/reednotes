A Memoir of Sorts
Updated June 2018
Mother never took to living near an oil refinery; it didn't bother me so much — I kind of liked it rather. We lived in a small town an hour north of San Francisco, the kind of town where everyone knew each other, and where everyday felt like a sunny afternoon. Mom and dad worked in the wine business, and every so often I got to tag along to Napa Valley or wherever. I loved it. Our little two story town house overlooked golden hills that rolled on for miles across the Carquinez Strait — the dry warm breeze, the dusty trails, the train that would encircle, and at nights at the town dock surrounded by palms, I would quietly sit and watch the stars. To this day, these are still among the happinest times in recent memory.
During summers, I took every opportunity to slink off to UC Berkeley. I loved auditing lectures, and this drove the attendance person crazy. The atmosphere at Berkeley imbued a sense of excitement about science and technology that has been with me ever since. By the time I matriculated to university, I had my eye so dead set on Silicon Valley that I didn't end up staying for very long. Around junior year, I got antsy, boarded on a plane to Boston and started a software company.
The company I started in my early twenties with my dear friend Will and Sergei has been the centerpiece of my life so far. The advent of HTML5 video presented an opportunity to link powerful object tracking and identification technology with in-video interaction. We scrambled to get working prototypes and to secure initial customer interest. Will was at Harvard at the time but got us funded anyway; he was a big source of our early momentum. Admitted I was very lucky to have had such wonderful partners and to have been in that area. During my stint, I served as Partner, President, and when Will went to Shanghai to set up a distribution arm, CEO. Our office eventually relocated to Los Angeles, where I served my remaining days. The people with whom I embarked on this journey and have met along the way are much the same people for whom I hold my deepest and warmest affection and admiration... They are very dear to me.
At any rate, these are the notes in my melodies. Some are ill-timed, most are serendipitous — all, I have found worthwhile.
Friends
Updated June 2018
Maybe it's trite to say that starting a company is a bit like going into battle, but I've certainly found the analogy apt. In the process, you are likey to discover the people who would stand by you when no one else would — in that sense, it's like a band of brothers. By no means is this list exhaustive, but here's a few I've had the good fortune of meeting:
Earliest memories I have were drafting and leaving it in Will's room a fake acceptance letter from Penn, and drifting fecklessly together in a snowy deserted parking lot. For me at least those days are as memorable any. Later both of us left school to start a company together. He pulled a lot of weight early on; truly, not enough credit can be attributed to him. There is no one more capable of carrying something out by sheer force of will than Will Joy (suitably).
Jeff Stern is one of the private investors in my company. He is one of the warmest people I have ever met and someone who I try to emulate daily. He exudes a level of persistence and charisma I've seen few people match. I learnt a lot from him of what it means to be on the periphery of a team, yet still have a very palpable presence.
I have probably spent more time with Serge Gmyria than anyone outside my family. As most people who knew me in my early twenties will tell you, I was an unruly kid. Those who had the misfortune of working for or with me had to put up with a lot of my nonsense. Sergie has endured more of it than anyone, and I "normalized" quite a bit as result of his friendship. It's hard to express my gratitude for how long he stuck it out with me and the sacrifices he's made. He is as kindhearted and thoughtful a friend as anyone could ask for.
As these things go, so much is tacit, and any glimmer is but a fraction of the whole.
